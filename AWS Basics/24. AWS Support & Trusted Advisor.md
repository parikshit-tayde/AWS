# AWS Support Plans & Trusted Advisor

## Overview — what it is and why it matters

AWS Support Plans define the SLA, response time, architectural guidance, and support channels available when your infrastructure needs help. Trusted Advisor is an automated tool that continuously reviews your account against best practices, flagging security, cost, reliability, performance, and quota issues before they become incidents.

Support plans are a cost lever many teams underspend on until they need it urgently. Trusted Advisor is often overlooked as a replacement for common sense, when it is actually the first filter that should eliminate entire classes of misconfiguration before support is ever needed.

---

## Simple explanation

Support plans are insurance. You pay a monthly fee and get access to AWS engineers who can help when you are stuck. Trusted Advisor is a continuous auditor who checks your account every day and flags obvious mistakes.

Think of Trusted Advisor as a checklist that runs automatically: Is MFA enabled on root? Are any security groups wide open? Is someone paying for resources that aren't in use? A good team runs Trusted Advisor weekly and fixes issues before they cause an incident. A bad team calls support to debug a problem that Trusted Advisor would have caught in 5 minutes.

## Key concepts

### AWS Support Plans

There are four support plans, each with increasing response time, guidance, and cost. The right plan depends on the criticality of the workload and the team's AWS expertise.

**Support Plan Comparison:**

| Dimension | Basic | Developer | Business | Enterprise |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cost | Free | 29/month (7% discount at higher volume) | 100+/month (10-15% of spending) | 15,000+/month + volume discount |
| Support channels | Email (account/billing) | Email support | Phone + email | Phone + email + dedicated TAM |
| General guidance response | 24 business hours | 12-24 hours | 1 hour (urgent) | 15 minutes (critical) |
| Production system down | Not covered | 12 hours | 1 hour | 15 minutes |
| Architectural guidance | Forum community only | Best effort (email) | Contextual guidance | Proactive guidance + reviews |
| Contacts | 1 (account root) | 1 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Trusted Advisor checks | 5 (free) | 5 (free) | 115 (all) | 115 (all) + proactive insights |
| AWS Support API | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| Infrastructure Event Mgmt | No | No | No | Yes (for planned events) |
| Quarterly business reviews | No | No | No | Yes |
| Use case | Free tier, learning, non-prod | Dev/test, low-priority prod | Standard production | Mission-critical, 24/7 scale |

**When to upgrade:**
- **To Developer** — if production is running but is not business-critical; one person can handle support email during business hours
- **To Business** — if one production outage costs more than the support plan annual cost; multiple team members need support access; full Trusted Advisor checks are needed
- **To Enterprise** — if 24/7 availability is contractual; a dedicated account manager (TAM) is worth the cost; AWS is a strategic partner

### Trusted Advisor — Automated Best Practices Auditor

Trusted Advisor runs checks against your account continuously. Issues are categorised into five pillars: Cost Optimization, Security, Reliability, Performance, Service Quotas.

**Free checks (available to all accounts):**

| Check | What it does | Common fix |
|---|---|---|
| Security Groups — Specific Ports Unrestricted | Finds security groups allowing 0.0.0.0/0 on dangerous ports (22, 3306, 5432, etc) | Restrict source IP to admin IP only |
| IAM Access Key Age | Flags keys not rotated in 90 days | Rotate key or delete unused key |
| MFA on Root Account | Checks if MFA is enabled on root user | Enable virtual MFA device on root |
| EBS Snapshots | Identifies snapshots older than a retention threshold | Delete snapshots beyond retention |
| Service Limits | Shows current usage against AWS account quotas | Request quota increase if approaching limit |

**Business/Enterprise checks (full Trusted Advisor, 115 total checks):**

| Category | Example checks | Business impact |
|---|---|---|
| Cost Optimization | Underutilized RDS instances, unattached Elastic IPs, unused CloudFront distributions, reserved instance opportunities, idle load balancers | Reduce monthly bill; often 5-20% savings |
| Security | Logging disabled on services, S3 bucket encryption, exposed DB snapshots, security group rules, KMS key rotation | Compliance, breach prevention |
| Reliability | Multi-AZ RDS, ALB health checks, Auto Scaling coverage, DynamoDB backups | Uptime SLA compliance |
| Performance | CloudFront compression, DynamoDB provisioning vs on-demand, RDS connection pooling, Aurora read replicas | Response time improvements |
| Service Quotas | All EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, API Gateway limits and current usage | Prevents quota-limit-induced failures |

**Trusted Advisor cost savings examples:**

| Finding | Typical monthly saving | ROI on Business support |
|---|---|---|
| Unattached Elastic IP (one) | 3.65 | 1% |
| Over-provisioned RDS (4 core → 2 core) | 1000-3000 | Months to pay |
| Unused CloudFront distributions (2-3) | 50-200 | Weeks to pay |
| Reserved instance opportunities (underutilized) | 500+ | Months to pay |
| Idle NAT Gateways (remove 1) | 32 | Days to pay |

### Trusted Advisor Dashboard and API

Access the dashboard in the AWS Console: **Support → Trusted Advisor Dashboard**

The dashboard shows:
- **Summary** — overview of all checks: green (passed), yellow (caution), red (action recommended)
- **Cost Optimization** — separate tab with all cost-related findings
- **Security** — security configuration findings
- **Reliability** — redundancy and failover configuration
- **Performance** — capacity and resource configuration
- **Service Quotas** — usage vs limits

**Refresh frequency:**
- **Free checks** — refresh every 24 hours
- **Business/Enterprise checks** — refresh as often as every 1 hour

**Programmatic access (Business/Enterprise only):**

```bash
# Get all Trusted Advisor checks
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-checks \
  --language en | jq '.checks | length'

# Get the result of a specific check (check ID varies; shown in describe output)
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-check-result \
  --check-id "CHECKID" \
  --language en \
  --query "checkResult.flaggedResources[*].metadata"
```

For automatic remediation workflows, Business/Enterprise customers often trigger Lambda functions based on Trusted Advisor findings via EventBridge.

## Lab — Explore Trusted Advisor Free Checks

### Goal

Access the Trusted Advisor dashboard, review the five free checks, identify at least one finding, and understand the remediation path.

### Steps

**Part 1 — Navigate to Trusted Advisor**

1. Open the AWS Console (ensure you are logged in with an account that has any level of AWS usage)
2. Navigate to **Support → Trusted Advisor Dashboard**
3. If this is the first visit, Trusted Advisor will refresh checks. Wait 30 seconds for the summary to populate.

**Part 2 — Review the Summary**

4. On the main Trusted Advisor page, observe the five check categories:
   - **Security Groups — Specific Ports Unrestricted** (green/yellow/red)
   - **IAM Access Key Age** (green/yellow/red)
   - **MFA on Root Account** (green/yellow/red)
   - **EBS Snapshots** (green/yellow/red)
   - **Service Limits** (green/yellow/red)
5. Note the colour coding: green = no findings, yellow = some attention needed, red = action recommended
6. Click **Refresh checks** to run an immediate re-check (wait for completion)

**Part 3 — Investigate a Finding**

7. Click on any check with a yellow or red status. The detail page shows:
   - Number of flagged resources
   - Specific resources flagged
   - Recommended remediation
8. Example: if "MFA on Root Account" is red, click it and follow the remediation link

**Part 4 — Take Action on One Finding**

9. Choose the simplest finding to remediate (e.g. rotate IAM access key, or enable MFA on root if not already enabled)
10. Follow the remediation step from Trusted Advisor
11. Return to the dashboard and click **Refresh checks**
12. Verify the finding is resolved (colour changes to green)

**Part 5 — Document Findings for a Team**

13. Screenshot or note all findings across the five checks
14. Create a simple remediation list:
    - Finding | Priority | Owner | Target date
15. This is the weekly Trusted Advisor review that production teams run

### CLI commands

```bash
# List all available Trusted Advisor check IDs
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-checks \
  --language en \
  --query "checks[*].[id, name]" \
  --output table

# Get a summary of all checks in the account
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-checks \
  --language en | jq '.checks | length' 

# Refresh a specific check (free checks auto-refresh; this is for Business/Enterprise)
aws support refresh-trusted-advisor-check \
  --check-id CHECKID

# Get flagged resources from a check
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-check-result \
  --check-id "CHECKID" \
  --language en \
  --query "checkResult.flaggedResources[0:5].metadata"

# Extract Security Groups check results
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-checks --language en | \
  jq '.checks[] | select(.name | contains("Security Groups"))'

# Get IAM Access Key Age findings
aws support describe-trusted-advisor-check-result \
  --check-id AKIA_KEY_CHECK_ID \
  --language en | jq '.checkResult'
```

## Common mistakes

**Assuming Trusted Advisor is a replacement for support.** Trusted Advisor flags configuration mistakes; it does not help with application bugs, architectural decisions, or novel problems. If TA finds nothing and the system is still broken, then support is needed.

**Not checking Trusted Advisor until there is an emergency.** Running TA weekly or monthly finds issues before they cause incidents. A proactive team eliminates 80% of potential issues through TA before they ever reach support.

**Ignoring yellow alerts.** Yellow means caution — not critical yet, but trending toward an issue. An old IAM key that hasn't been rotated in 90 days is yellow; if it's compromised, it's a red incident. Act on yellow.

**Staying on Basic support for production workloads.** Basic support has no SLA for production issues and no Trusted Advisor checks beyond the five free ones. If production is down, you get a best-effort response from a community forum. Business support guarantees 1-hour response on production-down issues and includes all 115 checks. The ROI is immediate for any non-trivial production workload.

**Not automating Trusted Advisor follow-up.** For teams managing many accounts (via AWS Organizations), TA findings should be automatically aggregated and pushed to a Slack channel or ticket system. Manually checking the dashboard weekly doesn't scale.

## Real-world use

A 40-person SaaS company runs on AWS. The DevOps engineer enables Business support and reviews Trusted Advisor weekly. In month 1, TA flags five underutilized RDS instances (dev/staging databases running at 2% CPU on db.r5.2xlarge). Downsizing to db.t4g.medium saves 6,500/month. In month 2, TA flags 12 unattached Elastic IPs, each charging 0.01/hour. Removal saves 105/month. In month 3, TA recommends reserved instances based on 90-day usage patterns. The company saves an additional 8,000/month by committing 1-year RIs to steady-state compute. Total first-quarter savings: 14,605/month. Business support cost: 1,500/month. ROI: 10x in the first quarter. Without Trusted Advisor, these costs would have continued indefinitely.

## Key takeaways

- Basic support (free) covers account and billing issues; production system outages are not covered
- Developer support (29/month) adds email support with 12-24h response; useful for non-critical production
- Business support (100+/month) adds phone support, 1-hour response on prod issues, and all 115 Trusted Advisor checks
- Enterprise support (15,000+/month) adds 15-minute response, dedicated Technical Account Manager, and proactive guidance
- Trusted Advisor's five free checks (security groups, IAM key age, MFA, EBS snapshots, service limits) catch 40% of common misconfigurations
- Business/Enterprise Trusted Advisor checks often pay for the support plan in savings within 1-3 months
- Trusted Advisor is a first-pass filter: run it weekly, fix findings, and only escalate to support when TA finds nothing but issues persist
